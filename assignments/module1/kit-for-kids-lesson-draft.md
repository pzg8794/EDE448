## 10-Minute Lesson Plan

**Lesson title:** Understanding and Supporting Autistic Classmates

**Grade band:** Grades 2-3, adaptable for other grade levels

**Objective:** Students will identify at least two respectful ways to include and support autistic classmates without assuming every autistic student needs the same support.

**Materials:**

- OAR Kit for Kids resource page
- OAR Kit for Kids Grades 2-3 Activity Workbook
- Optional board/chart paper
- Optional exit ticket: "One way I can be a supportive classmate is..."

**Lesson flow:**

1. Opening question, 1 minute
   Ask: "What does it mean to be a good classmate when someone learns, communicates, plays, or senses the world differently?"

2. Mini-reading or teacher explanation, 2 minutes
   Introduce the OAR Kit for Kids idea that students can be different and still share feelings, interests, friendships, and equal worth. Explain that autism can affect communication, learning, play, sensory experience, and social interaction, but autistic students are still classmates who deserve respect and belonging.

3. Same and different activity, 2 minutes
   Use the workbook's "same and different" idea. Ask students to name examples of ways classmates may be alike and different, such as interests, sensory preferences, communication styles, or ways of learning.

4. Supportive classmate discussion, 3 minutes
   Ask students to identify respectful supports:
   - invite without forcing
   - wait for someone to communicate
   - respect sensory tools, movement, or quiet time
   - do not tease someone for repeating words, avoiding eye contact, or needing a break
   - ask what helps instead of assuming
   - include classmates in group work, lunch, recess, and games

5. Exit reflection, 2 minutes
   Students complete or share: "One way I can support a classmate is..."
