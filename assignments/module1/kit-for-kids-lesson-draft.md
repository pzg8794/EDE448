## 10-Minute Lesson Plan

**Topic:** Understanding and supporting autistic classmates.

**Grade level:** **[insert grade]**

**Objective:** Students will identify at least two respectful ways to include and support autistic classmates without assuming every autistic student needs the same support.

**Materials:**

- OAR Kit for Kids resource: **[insert title]**
- Chart paper or whiteboard
- Optional exit ticket: "One way I can be a supportive classmate is..."

**Lesson flow:**

1. **Opening question, 1 minute:** "What does it mean to be a good classmate when someone learns, communicates, plays, or senses the world differently?"
2. **Resource mini-reading or visual, 3 minutes:** Use the OAR resource to introduce autism in student-friendly language.
3. **Discussion, 3 minutes:** Ask students to identify respectful supports, such as giving space, using kind words, inviting without pressuring, being patient with communication, and not making fun of sensory tools or differences.
4. **Application, 2 minutes:** Students name one classroom situation where inclusion matters: group work, lunch, recess, technology tasks, transitions, or partner work.
5. **Exit reflection, 1 minute:** Students complete or share: "One way I can support a classmate is..."
