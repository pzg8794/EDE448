# Module 1 Discussion Draft: What Do You Already Know?

## Prompt Focus

What do you already know about autism and other developmental disabilities?

## Draft Response

Before beginning this module, I understand autism and other developmental disabilities as differences that affect communication, sensory processing, social interaction, learning, movement, attention, and daily support needs in varied ways. I also know that no single description can capture every autistic person or every student with complex support needs. Some students may communicate through speech, AAC, gestures, movement, behavior, writing, or a combination of modes. Some may need substantial support with transitions, sensory regulation, routines, or social expectations while also having deep strengths, interests, pattern recognition, memory, creativity, or problem-solving abilities.

What I am trying to be careful about is the difference between knowing a diagnostic label and understanding a person. Labels can help students access services, but labels can also narrow how adults interpret them. If an adult sees autism only as a list of deficits, then behaviors like avoiding eye contact, scripting, needing movement, resisting a transition, or using AAC may be misread as noncompliance instead of communication or regulation.

My current lens is that developmental differences should be understood through both support needs and dignity. I want to learn how to design classrooms where communication differences, sensory needs, and social differences are planned for from the beginning. This connects to my broader Puzzle Plan work because educational systems create evidence about students. The evidence we choose to notice shapes the supports we build. If we notice only what students cannot do, we build control systems. If we notice access barriers, communication attempts, strengths, and context, we can build support systems that increase agency.

One question I am bringing into this module is: how can teachers balance accurate knowledge about autism and developmental disabilities with humility about each student's individual lived experience?
