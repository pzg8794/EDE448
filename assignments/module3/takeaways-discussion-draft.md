## Draft Response

My biggest takeaway from Module 3 is that meaningful communication has to be measured by agency. A student may have a communication tool, but the deeper question is whether the student can use it to influence their day, relationships, learning, and support. Communication should include choosing, refusing, commenting, asking questions, repairing misunderstandings, sharing feelings, participating academically, and building social connection.

This module also made me think differently about communication partners. AAC does not work only because a student has access to a device or board. It works when adults and peers model language, wait, respond, confirm meaning respectfully, and keep the system available across routines. Removing AAC, limiting vocabulary, or using it only during speech sessions can accidentally silence the student.

The Communication Support Plan helped me think about home-school communication as more than a behavior report. A daily log should document what the student communicated, what supports worked, what barriers appeared, and what the family wants school to know. That kind of log can become a bridge between settings instead of a list of problems.

For my future classroom, I want to treat communication support as part of Tier 1 inclusive design. Every classroom has students who need more than one way to understand, respond, ask for help, or participate. Building multiple communication pathways helps students with complex needs, but it also improves the classroom for everyone.
