# Module 3 Discussion Draft: What Do You Already Know?

## Prompt Focus

What do you already know about communication supports for students with complex needs?

## Draft Response

Before this module, I understand communication supports as much broader than speech. Students with complex needs may communicate through AAC, gestures, facial expression, body movement, eye gaze, behavior, vocalizations, writing, objects, pictures, or partner-assisted systems. A student does not stop communicating because adults do not understand the message. That means educators have a responsibility to build better listening systems.

I also know that AAC is not just a device. A device can be powerful, but communication access also depends on modeling, vocabulary, wait time, family knowledge, peer response, backup systems, and whether adults honor what the student communicates. If AAC is only used for requesting or answering adult questions, the student still does not have full communication access.

A question I am bringing into this module is how to make communication supports part of everyday classroom routines. I want to think about arrival, group work, transitions, lunch, recess, academic discussion, and dismissal. Students should be able to communicate needs, preferences, refusals, ideas, feelings, questions, and social comments across the day.

This connects to my course theme because communication is access-to-agency. If a student cannot affect what happens next, then the support system is not yet meaningful enough.
