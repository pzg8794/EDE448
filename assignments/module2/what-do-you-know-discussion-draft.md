# Module 2 Discussion Draft: What Do You Already Know?

## Prompt Focus

What do you already know about supporting prosocial norms, classroom behavior, and students with complex needs?

## Draft Response

Before this module, I understand classroom behavior support as something that should be proactive and relational rather than only reactive. Students need clear routines, predictable expectations, and supportive relationships, but they also need adults who are willing to ask what behavior is communicating. A behavior that looks disruptive may be connected to confusion, sensory overload, social stress, lack of communication access, fatigue, trauma, or a task that is not yet accessible.

I also know that classroom management can become harmful when it is only about adult control. For autistic and neurodivergent students, compliance-focused systems can punish communication, movement, refusal, sensory needs, or different forms of participation. I want to keep learning how to design support strategies that teach skills and protect the classroom community without forcing students to mask or erase their needs.

From my technology-rich classroom experiences, I have seen how students can appear off task when the barrier is actually hidden: login difficulty, unclear click paths, reading load, too many steps, group-role confusion, or fear of looking incapable. That makes me think behavior support has to include environmental design. If the system is confusing, students should not be the only ones treated as the problem.

My question for this module is: how can teachers create support systems that are structured enough to help the whole class, but flexible enough to respect dignity, consent, and neurodiversity?
