# Module 2: Supporting Students with Prosocial Norms in School-Based Settings - Comprehensive Themed Overview

## Big-Picture Frame

Module 2 shifts the course from understanding autism to designing support. Through the EDU498-style theme, behavior support should not become compliance training. The module asks how prevention, teaching, reinforcement, peer supports, classroom community, sensory supports, and communicative competence can protect dignity and agency for students misread by school systems.

## Essential Question

How can support strategies respect dignity, consent, and neurodiversity?

## Cross-Reading Synthesis

Across Module 2, the readings move from awareness to design. Positive support is not just a set of behavior strategies. It is a classroom architecture: prevention, teaching, reinforcement, peer support, classroom community, sensory access, communicative competence, and family/community partnership.

The module's strongest tension is evidence versus dignity. Evidence-based interventions matter, but they must be interpreted through dignity, consent, neurodiversity, and student agency. A plan that makes a student quieter is not automatically a just plan. A stronger plan asks whether the student has more ways to communicate, more access to peers, more predictable routines, more self-advocacy, and more belonging.

Through the EDU498 theme, behavior support becomes literacy work because students and adults are reading each other. The teacher reads behavior; the student reads expectations; peers read social cues; families read whether school understands their child. Module 2 asks us to design classrooms where those readings produce support instead of shame.

## Puzzle Plan / EQUITAS Synthesis

The module supports the Puzzle Plan by treating classroom evidence as a justice problem. If schools collect shallow evidence, students are misread. If schools collect richer evidence - communication attempts, sensory load, context, family knowledge, tool access, peer dynamics, and student preference - support becomes more accurate and humane.

EQUITAS asks us to audit the default learner. Across this module, the default learner is often assumed to be verbal, regulated, English-dominant, neurotypical, compliant, print-comfortable, and independently organized. The readings help us redesign for students whose communication, culture, bodymind, trauma history, language, disability, or support needs require something more flexible.

## Teaching Implications

- Name the actual access barrier instead of blaming the student.
- Build supports into everyday routines before crisis or failure.
- Use specific sources when writing assignments; do not cite vague module labels.
- Treat family knowledge and student communication as valid evidence.
- Connect every assignment back to dignity, agency, and participation.

## Final Module Takeaway

Module 2 teaches that positive support is only positive when it increases communication, dignity, consent, relationship, and participation.
