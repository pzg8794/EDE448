# EDE448 Alignment With PhD And Puzzle Plan Goals

## Why EDE448 Matters Strategically

EDE448 is a practice-facing course for the core Puzzle Plan question: how can systems interpret human difference without flattening it into deficit?

The course connects special education, AAC, positive behavior support, and sensory access to the same equity commitments that drive the broader PhD plan: diagnostic justice, inclusive technology, culturally responsive evidence, and tools that protect agency rather than simply sort people.

## Core Puzzle Plan Connection

The Puzzle Plan treats lived experience, technical systems, and educational practice as parts of one translational ecosystem. EDE448 contributes to that ecosystem by turning communication and behavior support into visible, reusable artifacts:

1. **Equitable interpretation.** Behavior is read as communication and context, not as moral failure.
2. **Communication access.** AAC, visual supports, home-school logs, and multimodal responses become core instructional infrastructure.
3. **Sensory and bodymind evidence.** Sensory barriers are treated as design data that can inform classroom change.
4. **Consent and dignity.** Support strategies are evaluated by whether they expand choice, belonging, and self-advocacy.
5. **Portfolio proof.** The final support portfolio can show a coherent teacher-scholar identity: data-informed, disability-justice centered, and practical enough for families and teachers to use.

## Proposed Course Research Question

> How can educators design communication and behavioral support systems that interpret autism and complex support needs through dignity, sensory access, AAC, family knowledge, and student agency rather than compliance alone?

## Personal Teaching Identity Link

This course should show Piter as a teacher-scholar who can:

- translate disability justice into daily classroom routines
- use AAC and visual supports as tools for authorship, not just classroom management
- distinguish skill gaps, access barriers, sensory load, linguistic load, and trauma/stress responses
- build support plans that include students, families, paraprofessionals, service providers, and teachers
- connect field-practice evidence from technology-rich classrooms to broader questions of diagnostic and educational equity
