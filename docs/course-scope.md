## Course Description In My Words

EDE448 prepares educators, counselors, and school leaders to understand disability through strengths-based, socially situated, and evidence-informed practice. The course focuses on students labeled with autism and other complex support needs, especially communication, AAC, behavior as communication, PBIS, proactive support, and family/interdisciplinary collaboration.

The central practical task is to build supports that help adults interpret students more carefully instead of treating communication differences, sensory needs, or behavior as problems to suppress.

## Learning Outcomes To Track

I will use this repo to document evidence that I can:

- recognize behavior as communication and socially constructed
- include students, families, team members, and communities in support planning
- promote choice-making, independence, self-advocacy, and meaningful participation
- use observation to define communication and behavioral support needs
- connect FBA/PTR/PBIS logic to responsive support plans
- design supports that are proactive, positive, respectful, age appropriate, and culturally responsive
- understand AAC and other communication systems as access tools, not last-resort interventions
- create learning environments that honor diversity, consent, bodymind access, and agency
