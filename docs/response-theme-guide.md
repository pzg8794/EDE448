# Response Theme Guide

Use this guide when drafting or revising any EDE448 assignment.

## Core Claim

Communication and positive behavior support are not separate classroom tasks. They are access systems. A student with autism or complex support needs is more likely to be understood when the classroom treats behavior, sensory responses, AAC use, silence, movement, family knowledge, and peer interaction as meaningful evidence.

## Course Throughline In One Sentence

EDE448 helps me move from asking "How do I manage this behavior?" to "What is this student communicating, what barrier is present, and what support would increase dignity, agency, and participation?"

## Reusable Reference Threads

### Puzzle Plan

Use when the assignment needs a broader PhD/research connection.

Claim to adapt:

> The Puzzle Plan treats lived experience, technical systems, and educational practice as connected evidence systems. In EDE448, that means classroom supports should collect richer evidence about communication, context, sensory access, and student agency instead of reducing students to deficit labels or isolated behaviors.

### Access-To-Agency

Use when the assignment asks about teaching practice or classroom design.

Claim to adapt:

> Access is not only placement in the room or possession of a tool. Access means the student can understand the routine, communicate needs, make choices, participate in academic/social life, and affect what happens next.

### Pine Brook / Technology-Rich Placement Evidence

Use when the assignment needs a concrete field-practice connection without identifying students.

Claim to adapt:

> In technology-rich classroom work, I have seen that student competence can be hidden by login friction, unclear click paths, reading load, role ambiguity, noise, peer pacing, or executive-function demands. That reminds me to interpret behavior as evidence of the task/environment fit, not only as evidence about the student.

### ED452C / Inclusive Design

Use when the assignment asks about proactive supports or planning.

Claim to adapt:

> Inclusive design means building supports into Tier 1 routines before a student is treated as individually failing. Visual schedules, role cards, sensory options, communication supports, and multiple response modes help teachers notice the difference between skill needs, access barriers, and behavior patterns.

### EDU486 / Technology And Agency

Use when the assignment involves AAC, classroom technology, or tools.

Claim to adapt:

> A tool is only inclusive if it expands agency. AAC, classroom technology, and visual supports should help students ask, refuse, comment, repair, choose, and participate, not simply make adult directions more efficient.

## Revision Checklist

- [ ] Does this draft answer the assignment prompt directly?
- [ ] Does it include the course theme without sounding pasted in?
- [ ] Does it connect to at least one reading, media source, or course resource?
- [ ] Does it include true, non-identifying lived/classroom detail where needed?
- [ ] Does it avoid treating compliance as the goal?
- [ ] Does it name how the support increases communication, agency, dignity, or participation?
- [ ] Does it preserve public/private boundaries?
