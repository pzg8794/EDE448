# EDE448 - Communication and Positive Behavioral Supports

Public course repository for **EDE 448: Communication and Positive Behavioral Supports for Students with Autism and Other Complex Needs**, Summer Session B 2026, Warner School of Education, University of Rochester.

This repo documents the course scope, deadlines, assignment work, portfolio planning, source trail, and strategic alignment with Piter Z. Garcia Bautista's broader PhD / Puzzle Plan goals: disability justice, equitable diagnostics, neurodivergent learning, communication access, inclusive technology, dignity-centered support, and educational systems that treat lived experience as evidence.

## Current Throughline

**Working course theme:** Communication as Access-to-Agency.

EDE448 is a direct bridge between special education practice and the Puzzle Plan. The course asks how educators understand autism and complex support needs, how behavior is interpreted as communication, and how AAC / positive behavior support can protect dignity, consent, identity, and student agency.

My working personal lens is:

> How can communication supports, sensory access, and positive behavioral systems help students with autism and complex support needs be understood as whole people whose behavior, language, sensory needs, culture, and technology access are all meaningful evidence?

## Public Repository Boundary

This repository is public. It intentionally tracks original planning, assignment drafts, source maps, and reflection work. Third-party readings, Blackboard exports, PDFs, DOCX source files, screenshots, private student data, health/disability details, and other copyrighted or private course resources stay local or in Google Drive.

Before any draft is submitted to Blackboard, verify the lived details: locations observed, students taught, tools tested, dates, citations, and any personally identifying details. The public draft files are built to make submission easier without inventing evidence.
